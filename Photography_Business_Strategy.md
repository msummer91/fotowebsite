# Photography Business Strategy
### Website · Instagram · Print Sales · Shipping
**For:** Markus Sommer — Switzerland-based photographer
**Portfolio:** Street/Urban · Landscape/Nature · Portrait/People
**Market:** Worldwide (EU printing)
**Budget:** €100–300/month | **Tech level:** Non-technical

---

## 1. Brand Foundation

### Naming Strategy

Your brand name is the single most important decision you'll make. It appears on your domain, your prints, your Instagram handle, and your packaging. Get it right before building anything else.

**Option A — Your full name**
`markussommer.com` / `@markussommerphoto`
Clean, personal, professional. Works especially well if you plan to do commissioned work alongside print sales. Many top photographers (Steve McCurry, Alex Strohl) build on their own name.

**Option B — A coined studio name**
Something evocative of your work: `SommerFrames`, `NordLichtPhoto`, `SommerVisuals`, `SommerStudios`
Gives you more flexibility if the business evolves, and can feel more "brand-like" on prints and packaging.

**Option C — A place/concept hook**
Something like `AlpineLens`, `HighgroundPhoto`, `GletscherLight` — leans into your Swiss base and landscape work.

**Recommendation:** For a mixed portfolio (street, landscape, portrait) with worldwide ambitions, **your full name or a clean studio name** is the safest long-term bet. Avoid anything too niche that locks you into one style.

### Visual Identity (DIY approach)

You do not need to hire a designer to start. Use **Canva** (free/pro ~€15/month) to create:

- A wordmark logo (your name in a clean serif or geometric sans-serif font)
- A consistent color palette: 2–3 colors maximum. Photographers typically use neutral/dark palettes (near-black, off-white, one accent) so the work stays center stage
- A consistent typography pair: one heading font + one body font
- A simple watermark for social posts (optional — some photographers prefer no watermark)

**Canva fonts that work well for photography brands:** Cormorant Garamond, Playfair Display, Helvetica Neue, Libre Baskerville

## 2. Website Strategy

### Platform Recommendation: Squarespace Commerce

For a non-technical photographer selling prints worldwide, **Squarespace** is the clear best choice. It combines the best portfolio templates in the industry with a fully functional e-commerce engine, and it integrates directly with your print fulfillment partner.

**Why Squarespace over alternatives:**
- Drag-and-drop — no code ever needed
- Purpose-built photography templates (full-bleed layouts, galleries, lightbox)
- Built-in storefront with physical product support
- Connects natively with Prodigi (your EU print fulfillment partner — see Section 4)
- Stripe payments built in — works in Switzerland
- SSL, hosting, CDN all included
- ~€26/month on the Commerce Basic plan

**Alternatives worth knowing:** Format (~€18/month, photography-focused but smaller ecosystem), Pixieset (~€25/month, excellent for photographers but less flexible on design), SmugMug (~€33/month, strong print sales engine).

### Website Structure

Keep it simple. You only need 6 pages to launch:

**1. Home**
Full-screen hero image, your name, one-line tagline, and two clear calls to action: "View Portfolio" and "Shop Prints." Rotate hero images seasonally.

**2. Portfolio / Gallery**
Organized into 3 galleries:
- Landscape & Nature
- Street & Urban
- Portraits

Use Squarespace's built-in gallery blocks. Keep each gallery to your 15–25 strongest images. Edit ruthlessly — quality beats quantity every time.

**3. Shop**
Your print store. Products organized by category and/or format (Fine Art Prints, Framed Prints, Canvas). Clear product photography, size options, and pricing. More detail in Section 4.

**4. About**
Your story in 150–250 words. Who you are, where you're based (Switzerland is a selling point), what drives your photography. One strong portrait of you. This page builds trust and connection — it sells prints.

**5. Contact**
Simple contact form. Email address. Instagram link. Optionally a press/licensing inquiry option if you're open to commercial use of your images.

**6. FAQ / Shipping Info**
Answers to: print sizes, paper types, framing options, shipping times by region, return policy, and print quality details. Reduces customer service emails significantly.

### Domain Registration

Register your domain through **Namecheap** (~€12/year for .com) or directly through Squarespace. Priority order for extensions: `.com` → `.photo` → `.ch` (add .ch as a secondary redirect if you want it).

### SEO Basics

Squarespace has decent built-in SEO. Do these things on day one:
- Write a meta description for every page (2–3 sentences)
- Name your image files descriptively before uploading (`zurich-night-street-photography.jpg`, not `IMG_4521.jpg`)
- Add alt text to every image
- Set your site language to English (primary market) with German in the About/Contact if relevant

## 3. Print Product Strategy

### What to Sell

Start with a focused, manageable product range. You can expand later. A strong launch lineup:

**Tier 1 — Fine Art Giclée Prints (unframed)**
Your core product. High-margin, easy to ship, the most popular format for art collectors.
- Sizes: A4 (21×30cm), A3 (30×42cm), A2 (42×59cm), 50×70cm, A1 (59×84cm)
- Paper: Hahnemühle Photo Rag 308gsm or Canson Baryta 310gsm (offered through Prodigi)
- Editions: Consider limited editions (e.g., 50 prints per image) — adds perceived value and urgency

**Tier 2 — Framed Prints**
Higher price point, higher perceived value, more complex to ship. Add once you're established with unframed prints.

**Tier 3 — Canvas Prints**
Popular for interior decoration. Prodigi produces these at good quality.

**What NOT to sell at launch:** Mugs, phone cases, t-shirts. They dilute your brand. Focus on wall art.

### Pricing Framework

Photography prints are a premium product. Don't race to the bottom. Price based on perceived value, not just cost.

**Formula:** Production cost × 3.5–5x = retail price

**Example pricing (fine art print, A3 size):**
- Production cost via Prodigi: ~€12–18 (incl. shipping to customer)
- Your retail price: €59–79
- Your margin: ~€40–60 per print

**Example pricing by size:**

| Size | Retail Price (CHF/EUR) |
|------|----------------------|
| A4 | CHF 49 / €45 |
| A3 | CHF 79 / €72 |
| A2 | CHF 129 / €118 |
| 50×70cm | CHF 149 / €136 |
| A1 | CHF 199 / €182 |
| Framed A3 | CHF 149 / €136 |
| Framed A2 | CHF 229 / €210 |

Adjust based on your actual Prodigi costs once you've placed test orders. Always test print every image before listing it for sale.

### Image Curation for the Shop

Not every portfolio image belongs in the shop. Choose images that:
- Work beautifully large (fine detail, strong composition)
- Have broad appeal (aspirational locations, strong emotion, timeless quality)
- Are technically flawless at 100% zoom (no noise issues, perfect sharpness)

Start with 15–25 images in your shop. Add new work quarterly.

## 4. Print Fulfillment: EU Printing & Worldwide Shipping

### Recommended Partner: Prodigi

**Prodigi** (prodigi.com) is your best option. Here's why it fits your situation perfectly:

- **EU-based labs:** Prodigi routes orders to labs in the Netherlands, Germany, and the UK — keeping shipping within the EU fast and cost-effective
- **Worldwide delivery:** Ships to 140+ countries from EU labs
- **Fine art quality:** Offers Hahnemühle, Canson, and Breathing Color paper stocks — the same papers used by top-tier galleries
- **No monthly fee:** You pay per order only (production cost + shipping)
- **Squarespace integration:** Direct API connection — orders placed on your website are automatically sent to Prodigi for fulfillment. Zero manual work.
- **Test print program:** Order samples at cost before listing any product

**How the end-to-end flow works:**
1. Customer visits your Squarespace website
2. Customer selects an image, size, and paper type — adds to cart
3. Customer checks out, pays via Stripe
4. Squarespace automatically forwards the order to Prodigi via the integration
5. Prodigi prints at their nearest EU lab and ships directly to your customer
6. Customer receives tracking info automatically
7. You receive payment minus Squarespace's transaction fee (0% on Commerce Basic)

You never touch the print. No inventory. No packaging. No trips to the post office.

**Alternative:** **Printful** (has EU fulfillment in Latvia and Spain, slightly simpler setup, slightly lower fine art paper quality options, but excellent for canvas and framed prints).

### Important: Switzerland VAT & Customs

Since you're based in Switzerland (outside the EU), understand these rules:

**For EU customers:** Your prints are produced and shipped from within the EU by Prodigi, so EU VAT is charged at the point of sale. Prodigi handles this as the "seller of record" in most configurations — verify this with Prodigi's support.

**For Swiss domestic sales:** Switzerland has its own VAT (currently 8.1%). If your annual turnover stays below CHF 100,000, you are not required to register for Swiss VAT. Track this carefully.

**Strongly recommended:** Consult a Swiss-based accountant or Treuhänder before you start making significant sales. Cross-border e-commerce tax has real complexity. The cost of an hour of advice saves much larger headaches later.

## 5. Payment Setup

### Recommended: Squarespace Payments (powered by Stripe)

Built directly into Squarespace — zero additional setup. Supports:

- All major credit and debit cards (Visa, Mastercard, Amex)
- Apple Pay and Google Pay (critical for mobile conversions)
- PayPal (add as secondary option — many buyers prefer it)
- Multi-currency display (show prices in CHF, EUR, USD, GBP)

**Transaction fees:** 0% Squarespace fee on Commerce Basic + Stripe's standard rate (~1.5% + €0.25 for European cards, ~2.9% + €0.25 for international cards).

**Setting up in Switzerland:** Stripe is fully available to Swiss residents. You'll need:
- A Swiss bank account (or Swiss IBAN)
- Your Swiss ID/passport
- A business registration is not strictly required initially, but a Einzelfirma (sole proprietorship) is easy to set up and recommended once you're generating consistent income

### Currency Strategy

Display prices in CHF as your base currency (since you're Swiss), and enable automatic currency conversion for EUR, USD, GBP, and AUD. Squarespace + Stripe handles this automatically. Tip: round your prices to clean numbers in each currency (€79 not €73.42).

## 6. Instagram Strategy

### Profile Setup

**Handle:** `@[yourname]photo` or `@[yourstudioname]` — keep it consistent with your website domain.

**Bio formula (150 characters max):**
```
Fine art photography from Switzerland 🇨🇭
Street · Landscape · Portraits
Prints available worldwide → link
```

**Link in bio:** Use **Linktree** (free) or Squarespace's built-in link page to point to: Shop, Portfolio, About. Single most important link.

**Profile image:** Your face, not a logo. People connect with people first.

**Story Highlights:** Set up 5 highlights from day one — even if empty at launch:
- "Prints" (link to shop)
- "Behind the Lens" (process/BTS)
- "Landscape"
- "Street"
- "Portraits"

### Content Pillars

Your Instagram needs content variety to stay interesting and drive different types of engagement. Build your calendar around 4 pillars:

**Pillar 1 — The Work (50% of posts)**
Your strongest images. No caption needed beyond a brief scene description and location. Let the photography speak. Use carousel posts to show multiple perspectives of the same shoot.

**Pillar 2 — The Story (25% of posts)**
Behind the scenes, your process, the moment behind the image. "This was shot at 5:30am in Zurich's old town after waiting 40 minutes for the fog to settle just right." This content builds emotional connection and is what turns followers into buyers.

**Pillar 3 — Print & Product (15% of posts)**
In-situ shots of your prints on walls (get friends/family to display them and photograph the result), unboxing moments, limited edition announcements, new arrivals in the shop. Make buying feel aspirational.

**Pillar 4 — Community & Dialogue (10% of posts)**
Polls, questions, "which one" choices between two images, asking for caption ideas. Drives engagement and algorithmic reach.

### Posting Cadence

**Starting out (first 3 months):** 4–5 posts per week on the main feed. Daily Stories.

**Once established:** 3–4 feed posts per week + 5–7 Stories per week + 2–3 Reels per week.

**Best posting times (Central European Time):** 7–9am, 12–2pm, 7–9pm. Test and adjust based on your own analytics after 4–6 weeks.

**Reels:** Instagram's algorithm currently heavily favors Reels. Create short (15–30 second) clips: time-lapses, before/after edits, "a day shooting in X city." These drive far more discovery than static posts.

### Hashtag Strategy

Use 5–10 highly relevant hashtags rather than 30 generic ones. Mix levels:

**Niche/specific (easiest to rank in):**
`#switzerlandphotography` `#zurichstreetphotography` `#swisslandscape` `#alpsphoto` `#portraitphotographyswiss`

**Medium (competitive but reachable):**
`#streetphotography` `#landscapephotography` `#fineart photography` `#printsforsale` `#photographyprints`

**Large (for exposure, low conversion):**
`#photography` `#photooftheday`

Rotate your hashtag sets. Don't use the exact same set on every post.

### Growth Levers

**Early growth tactics:**
- Follow and genuinely engage with other photographers in your niches (comment meaningfully, not just emoji)
- Tag locations in every post — geotagged content gets discovered locally
- Collaborate with interior design accounts — your target buyers often follow them
- Feature your prints in styled room settings — interior/home decor content performs exceptionally well for print sales
- Share to your personal network for initial momentum

**Longer-term:**
- Instagram Shopping: Link your Squarespace product catalog to Instagram so people can buy directly from posts. Set this up once you have 20+ products.
- Occasional paid promotion: €20–50 per post to targeted audiences (people who follow art, interior design, travel) in key markets (Germany, UK, Netherlands, USA) can be very efficient

## 7. Content Creation Strategy

### Photography Workflow

**Editing pipeline:** Lightroom (Classic or Cloud, ~€12/month) → export masters → export web-optimized versions (2048px longest edge, sRGB, 85% JPEG quality for web; maximum resolution TIFF/PSD for print uploads to Prodigi).

**Naming convention:** `[year]-[month]-[location]-[subject]-[sequence].jpg`
Example: `2024-03-zurich-niederdorf-street-014.jpg`

**Color profiles:** Always deliver prints in Adobe RGB or ProPhoto RGB (Prodigi accepts both). Your website images should be in sRGB.

### Content Calendar Template

Build a simple rolling 4-week calendar. Each week covers:

- Monday: Portfolio image (Pillar 1) + Story with BTS
- Wednesday: Story/Process post (Pillar 2)
- Friday: Print/Product post or new image drop (Pillar 3)
- Saturday/Sunday: Reel or carousel from a recent shoot

Batch your content creation. Pick one day per week or fortnight to plan and schedule 2 weeks of posts in advance using **Later** (free plan works well for starting out, ~€18/month for pro).

### Photography Trips & Seasonal Content

Plan your shooting calendar around high-engagement seasons and locations:

- **Spring (Mar–May):** Alpine blossoms, melting snow, golden hour in Swiss cities
- **Summer (Jun–Aug):** Mountain peaks, lake reflections, travel to European cities for street work
- **Autumn (Sep–Nov):** Peak foliage, mist in cities, moody urban shots (huge for print sales)
- **Winter (Dec–Feb):** Snow scenes, Christmas markets, blue hour cityscapes

These seasonal drops give you natural hooks for new product announcements.

## 10. Key Success Principles

**Edit ruthlessly.** 15 exceptional images in your shop will outsell 100 mediocre ones. The quality of what you sell is your reputation.

**Show the prints in context.** Mockup tools (Canva, Printful's mockup generator, or Placeit) let you create lifestyle imagery showing your prints hung on beautiful walls. This is the single biggest driver of print sales online.

**Be patient with growth.** Instagram typically takes 6–12 months of consistent posting before organic growth becomes noticeable. The photographers who succeed commit to this without expecting overnight results.

**Build an email list early.** Add a simple email signup to your website ("Be first to know about new prints and limited editions"). Email subscribers convert to buyers at 5–10× the rate of social followers. Mailchimp is free up to 500 subscribers.

**Protect your work.** Watermark your social media images lightly (bottom corner, subtle). For Squarespace, disable right-click image saving (Squarespace has a setting for this). Register your best images with a copyright registry if selling at significant volume.

**Stay consistent, not perfect.** A website that's 85% of what you imagined and live beats a perfect one that never launches. Ship it. Improve it over time.
